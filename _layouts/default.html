Admitting
the need for change

It seems now that people admit that they do not have the time, energy or capacity to change their behaviour into something that is not directly incentivized or forced. They also admit they do not comprehend how the world could be changed and what exactly needs to be done. They say they are by nature lazy, but in fact they are not. They are the offspring of an old and life-blind economic system that has used them and tiered them out. This system has shaped their minds and their actions, and the key to change is to change the system.
